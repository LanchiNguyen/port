Mug

A reusable-cup rewards app built to make sustainability more convenient for coffee drinkers and more valuable for local cafes.

Project Info

ClientGroup project
RoleProduct designer
Timeline3 months
ToolsFigma, Whimsical
SkillsUser research, product thinking, UI/UX mobile design, interaction design, brand design
Problem

How do you make the greener option feel easier, not harder?

Mug is a mobile app that incentivizes people to bring reusable mugs by rewarding sustainable behavior at local coffee shops.

The product was grounded in a simple tension: users and local cafes are often uninformed or unmotivated to reduce single-use cup waste, even though the environmental impact is significant.

Research

What keeps people from changing their habits?

Our team surveyed 60 college students who did not use reusable cups. The biggest blockers were forgetting to bring cups, not seeing enough personal reason to switch, and not having time.

That led us to a broader framing: the app had to reward behavior, surface nearby cafes, and make sustainable choices fit seamlessly into an already rushed daily routine.

The design opportunity wasn’t just “make it green.” It was “make it effortless enough to survive a chaotic morning.”
Concept

A social rewards system for sustainable coffee habits

I helped shape features around faster ordering, mobile payment, personal avatars, reusable-mug tracking, and local cafe discovery. The final concept also introduced ECOmmunity, a social layer where users could see one another’s progress and stay accountable.

As the product direction matured, I also created the brand identity. We chose a color system that felt calm, cheerful, and sustainability-forward, anchored by Recoleta Bold and Montserrat Alternates for a friendly but readable tone.

Solution

Simple onboarding, fast access, visible impact

The high-fidelity prototype focused on clarity: a straightforward sign-up flow, a home screen that surfaces cups saved and local recommendations, and a central payment barcode for speed in-store.

ECOmmunity extended the product beyond utility by making sustainable behavior feel social and rewarding. That combination of convenience, motivation, and local business support became the project’s core value.
