Health-tech UX

Hey Period

Building a trustworthy, energetic health brand across user research, a marketing website, and a companion mobile interface.

Project Info

ClientHey Period
RoleSole product designer
Timeline2 months
ToolsFigma
SkillsUser research, brand design, web design, mobile design
Summary

Making emerging science feel credible and inviting

Hey Period is a healthcare startup enabling women to track their health through menstrual blood testing.

To build a waitlist while the product was still in R&D, the startup needed a fresher, more trustworthy brand. I partnered with the Head of Product to create the identity, marketing site, and mobile visual design.

Research

Trust first, then personality

I started by interviewing women about their experiences with period tracking and healthcare products. The target users were women ages 18 to 35 who wanted better ways to monitor their health.

The biggest design tension became clear quickly: the brand needed to feel medically credible without becoming cold or sterile. Users wanted trust, but they also wanted a sense of playfulness and community.

System

Brand identity, website, and app visuals in one language

I translated those insights into mood boards and a visual direction built around a vivid palette, energetic shapes, and Poppins typography. The result felt youthful and distinctive while remaining easy to read and emotionally familiar.

Once the direction was clear, I designed the marketing website to support waitlist growth and worked with the Head of Product to refine the feature story and supporting assets. I also updated the mobile interface from low-fidelity concepts into a high-fidelity prototype aligned with the new brand.

Reflection

Designing inside startup volatility

This project taught me adaptability. Hey Period’s priorities changed constantly, and I had to keep the work useful even as the direction shifted week to week.

Because the team had limited time and budget, I also learned to lean on secondary research and tighter collaboration to keep the design grounded. Even with those constraints, it was exciting to help shape a brand at the edge of science before it reached market.
